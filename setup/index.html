Setup Tips From Experience

Light Your Print Area First

Before you buy another spool of filament, light your workspace properly. A $15 LED strip improves print monitoring more than a $100 upgrade to your printer. I'm not exaggerating.

Track Your Humidity

If your PETG is stringing and your nylon is failing, check your humidity first. Most people skip this and spend weeks "tuning" when their filament is just wet. $15 sensor, problem solved.

Use Neutral White for Photos

4000-5000K for content, 6500K for inspecting prints. Warm light (2700K) hides flaws and makes colors look wrong on camera. Your timelapses will thank you.

Don't Sleep on Build Plates

The Biqu Glacier is my most-viewed video topic because it's the highest-impact, lowest-cost upgrade you can make. Swap your stock plate, thank me later.
